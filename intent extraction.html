Automated entity and intent extraction

Rasa Open Source has effectively simplified the process of creating a chatbot. Once we get familiar with the multitude of functionalities like intents, entities, stories, slots and actions; it becomes a matter of understanding when to apply each of them to obtain the required result. Although rasa has revolutionised the process of building functional chatbots, there is no way to train the chatbot without manually entering the training data.

Figure: Rasa chatbot

In root2AI we sought to find a way to automatically read PDF files and extract intents and entities for the same, these will become the training data for the chatbot.

To achieve our goal, we began to build an NLP framework capable of understanding the contextual meaning of the text and extract training data accordingly. The data was extracted, custom Named Entity Recognition was performed and a labelled dataset was used to train a Recurrent Neural Network.

These were the steps used to develop such a framework:

Identifying domain

Before we begin creating an NLP framework, it is pertinent we recognise the importance of working with a domain-specific text. Having text belonging to a certain domain of topic reduces the need for a wide range of entities. This in turn affects the extraction of entities and training of models. The models will also be able to understand the context of the text much better and identify the intents with accurate contextual meaning.

Extracting and cleaning the data

  • Extract text from PDF
    Pdfminer is a tool for extracting text from a PDF. It is effective in extracting formatted text without altering the layout and specific pages along with supporting basic encryption. The PDFminer also enables us to extract text out of both column-wise and row-wise formatted PDFs with no hassle. Using this tool, we were able to accurately extract all text from the PDF into a text document.
  • Clean text
    To be able to perform a custom NER on the text, it is essential to clean the text. Removing stop words, special characters and unnecessary characters and appending the whole document into one strong (removing formatting) will be the necessary format.

NER on the text - forming training dataset

  • Custom NER using spacy
    Named Entity Recognition is a part of information extraction that identifies specific entities from text. It helps us understand the types of entities in the text. Spacy is an open-source library that offers advanced NER features and also the option of forming our own custom entities. Using this feature, we can identify all the entities from a certain text.
    • Figure: Spacy NER Architecture. Language processing pipeline
  • Spacy also lets us define our custom entities
    In this case, the entities domain-specific Forming the training dataset- The custom NER training of the spacy model on our text will help us formulate a training dataset on which our neural network model will be able to detect entities. Identifying the domain-specific entities, the corresponding sentences and parts of speech of the said entity will form our training data.

Training the dataset -using neural networks

  • Choosing an information retrieval vectoriser
    There are many methods such as Latent Semantic Analysis(LSA) or Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) or TF-IDF help us understand the major topics in a document. On understanding each of these vectorisers separately, TF-IDF produces results of much more accuracy. Since it takes into account both the occurrence of the word in one document and its occurrences through all documents, TF_IDF produces context-specific results. Using the same, we identify the major topics and entities in our text- this will give us a sufficient idea about the entities we train.
    • Figure: Graphical representation of LDA with annotations. Source: Hong 2018.
  • LSTM
    Long Term Short Memory are a type of recurrent neural networks that are capable of selective remembering and forgetting that make them more effective in problems such as these. Being bidirectional, it can go back and forth between the text and identify entities while understanding the context to a higher level. The LSTM model identifies the labels for every sentence and trains the model, which is then used to predict the entities of test sentences later.

This gives us a framework that can identify the entities without having to specify the examples. Employing this framework gives us an easier way of producing bot responses for the entities so identified. Adding this feature to a rasa chatbot will increase the efficiency of the bot and reduce the time spent on manually specifying training data for the bot.
