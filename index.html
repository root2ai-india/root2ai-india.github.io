Implementation Approach

Our analytics projects naturally follows this path to achive a bechmark results.

01. Defining the business needs

The first stage in the business analytics process involves understanding what the business would like to improve on or the problem it wants solved.

02. Explore the data

This stage involves cleaning the data, making computations for missing data, removing outliers, and transforming combinations of variables to form new variables.

03. Analyse the data

Using statistical analysis methods such as correlation analysis and hypothesis testing, the analyst will find all factors that are related to the target variable.

04. Predict what is likely to happen

We model the data using predictive techniques that include decision trees, neural networks and logistic regression.

05. Optimise (find the best solution)

We apply the predictive model coefficients and outcomes to run ‘what-if’ scenarios, using targets set by managers to determine the best solution, with the given constraints and limitations.

06. Make a decision and measure the outcome

Make decisions and take action based on the derived insights from the model and the organisational goals

07. Update the system with the results of the decision

Finally the results of the decision and action and the new insights derived from the model are recorded and updated into the database
